We will be part of the radical hospitality that is Glitter Ash Wednesday

By being marked with ashes and glitter, we join with others who have participated in the public witness of Glitter Ash Wednesday across the U.S., the U.K., and Canada since its beginnings in 2017.

Glitter Ash Wednesday imagines a world of transformation, courage, and resistance and invites us all to bear the mark of inclusion and hope.

What is Glitter Ash Wednesday?

Glitter Ash Wednesday is a special type of Ash Wednesday service created especially by and for the transgender and LGBTQ+ community and allies. Worshippers are offered a mixture of ashes and glitter (or only ashes, if desired). You can be marked on your forehead with a cross or, alternatively, a circle, symbolizing the circle of life and death.

The ritual offers Christians an invitation to show up, or "come out," as faith affirming and LGBTQ+ affirming.

What does it mean?

The ritual captures the relationship between Ash Wednesday and Easter, which is the relationship between death and new life. In Glitter Ash Wednesday services, the sparkle of glitter with ashes represents the mix of hope with the ashes of death. Ash with glitter shows that in these times of death-dealing forces such as racial injustice and white supremacy, unimaginable wealth inequality, and climate crisis, we reject despair and look to light and hope.

Glitter Ash Wednesday embraces the power and solemnity of traditional Ash Wednesday rituals and is not a critique of those traditions. Glitter Ash Wednesday centers the LBGTQ+ community in the ritual, recognizing that many Christian traditions have attempted to exclude queer people from Christianity or have perpetrated harm against the LGBTQ+ community. When we participate in Glitter Ash Wednesday we proudly stand in the long tradition of Christians who have fought righteously for the liberation of oppressed people. With this ritual we joyfully claim the power of radical inclusivity and the power of allies standing in solidarity with targeted communities.

Because Glitter Ash Wednesday is all about stepping up, you can commit to a Lenten Action: a specific action, chosen by you, to promote resurrection. You can choose to put energy into a cause that promotes life and health in the face of death-dealing forces in our world.

Who can participate?

Everyone is welcome. You do not need to be Christian or a member of the LGBTQ+ community to participate.
