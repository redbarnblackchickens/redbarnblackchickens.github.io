We will be part of the radical hospitality that is Glitter Ash Wednesday.

By being marked with ashes and glitter, we join with others who have articipated in the public witness of Glitter Ash Wednesday across the U.S., the U.K., and Canada since its beginnings in 2017.

Glitter Ash Wednesday imagines a world of transformation, courage, and resistance and invites us all to bear the mark of inclusion and hope.

What is Glitter Ash Wednesday?

Glitter Ash Wednesday is a special type of Ash Wednesday service designed especially for the transgender and LGBTQ+ community. Worshippers are offered a mixture of ashes and glitter (or only ashes, if they wish). They can choose to be marked on their forehead with a cross or, alternatively, a circle, symbolizing the circle of life and death.

The ritual offers Christians an invitation to show up, or "come out," as faith affirming and LGBTQ+ affirming.

What does it mean?

The ritual captures the relationship between Ash Wednesday and Easter, which is the relationship between death and new life. In Glitter Ash Wednesday services the sparkle of glitter with ashes represents the mix of hope with the ashes of death. Ash with glitter shows that in these times of death-dealing forces such as racial injustice and white supremacy, unimaginable wealth inequality, and climate crisis, we reject despair and look to light and hope.

This event is not a critique of traditional Ash Wednesday rituals or services. The ritual is centered on the LBGTQ+ community in recognition that many Christian traditions have attempted to exclude the LGBTQ+ community from Christianity or have perpetrated harm against that community. Glitter Ash Wednesday also proudly stands in the long tradition of Christians who have fought righteously for the liberation of oppressed people. With this ritual we joyfully claim the power of radical inclusivity, and the power of allies standing in solidarity with targeted communities, in making change.

All people are welcome to be part of this event. Participants need not be Christian or LGBTQIA to participate.

Consider choosing a Lenten action

Ash Wednesday is the beginning of the season of Lent. It’s common for people to “give something up” for Lent. It is also entirely appropriate to take something on for Lent. Usually this is called a Lenten Discipline.

Because Glitter Ash Wednesday is all about stepping up, you can commit to a Lenten Action: a specific action, chosen by you, to promote resurrection. You can choose to put energy into a cause that promotes life and health in the face of death-dealing forces in our world.

Who can participate?

All trans-positive and queer-positive people who value inclusive Christianity are welcome. You do not need to be Christian or a member of the LGBTQ+ community to participate.
