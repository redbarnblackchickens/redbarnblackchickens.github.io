Afton Indivisible

March 2 is Glitter Ash Wednesday in Hastings

Glitter Ash Wednesday started in 2017 as an opportunity for queer and queer-positive Christians to celebrate the inclusion of trans and LGBTQ+ people in their faith traditions.

“Glitter Ash Wednesday shows that despite the message of hate LGBT people hear from some religious people, there are many more who love LGBT people, welcome them to their churches, and affirm their spiritual gifts,” says Rev. Marian Edmonds-Allen, Executive Director of Parity, the organizer of Glitter Ash Wednesday. “It is a way to ‘come out’ as an affirming Christian.”

"Spotlight on Democracy" Rally at the MN Capitol

On January 6, 2022, with temperatures well below zero, we with joined with over a hundred Twin Cities Indivisible activists and friends at the Minnesota Capitol for a nationwide night focused on democracy and upholding the right to vote.

This event was one of over 200 across the country to advocate for the passage of voting rights legislation.

Rally to Support Transgender Kids

In December 2021 Afton Indivisible coordinated a coalition of local and statewide organizations to show love and support for transgender, non-binary, and LGBTQ+ children at a rally in nearby Hastings, MN, that drew 900 people.

The rally was a direct response to the recent national news that Hastings School Board Chair Kelsey Waits was bullied and harassed throughout her recent bid for re-election.

We will not be divided by attacks against anyone’s family or anyone’s kids — no exceptions, trans and nonbinary and LGBTQ kids included.

We reject division, hate, and fear-mongering and rallied to show we embrace kindness, respect, and civil discourse.

Afton/St. Croix Valley Policing Series

Against the backdrop of a national focus on policing, Afton Indivisible takes a local look at policing and mental health in our community.

In addition to organizing, voter education, and get-out-the-vote efforts, here's some of what we've been up to.

A conversation with Washington County Sheriff Dan Starry

What is the police response to mental health crises in our community? What are local policing priorities? We spoke with Washington County Sheriff Dan Starry about crisis intervention training, opioid reentry programs, and support for mental health resources in our community.

Crisis Intervention Training for Cops

Afton Indivisible member Lucia Wroblewski served over 28 years as a St. Paul police officer and has a passion for good policing. She presented an inside look at coaching St. Paul police to deescalating mental health crises.
