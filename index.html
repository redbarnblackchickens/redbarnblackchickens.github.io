Rally to Support Trans Kids

Saturday, December 4, 1:00 – 2:00 PM
Levee Park Rotary Pavilion
20 Ramsey Street, Hastings, MN 55033
View map
The Facebook event for the rally is here.

Afton Indivisible is coordinating a coalition of local and statewide organizations that will gather to show love and support for transgender, non-binary, and LGBTQ+ children. A rally will be held at the Rotary Pavilion in Levee Park, 20 Ramsey Street, in Hastings, on Saturday, December 4th from 1 to 2 p.m. The public is encouraged to attend to hear speakers, share support, and call on city leaders to denounce bigotry and transphobia in Hastings.

The rally is in direct response to the recent national news that Hastings School Board Chair Kelsey Waits was bullied and harassed throughout her recent bid for re-election. These attacks went well beyond the typical criticism and scrutiny that elected officials often face. Kelsey’s youngest child, a second-grader, was outed as transgender by a local group of parents who had activated around the election.

Afton Indivisible stands with trans kids and their families. Attacks on families for political gain are off limits and cannot be part of civil discourse.

We will not be divided by attacks against anyone’s family or anyone’s kids — no exceptions, trans and nonbinary and LGBTQ kids included.

We reject division, hate, and fear-mongering and are rallying to show we embrace kindness, respect, and civil discourse.

Expected attendees and supporters include Hastings Progressives; RECLAIM; ACLU of Minnesota; Afton Indivisible; St. Croix Valley Indivisible; Indivisible St. Paul; MN Indivisible Alliance; Hastings THRIVE; IDEA: Inclusion, Diversity & Equality Alliance; Transforming Families; Hastings civic, business, non-profit, and faith leaders; and more.

Afton/St. Croix Valley Policing Series

Against the backdrop of a national focus on policing, Afton Indivisible takes a local look at policing and mental health in our community.

In addition to organizing, voter education, and get-out-the-vote efforts, here's some of what we've been up to.

A conversation with Washington County Sheriff Dan Starry

What is the police response to mental health crises in our community? What are local policing priorities? We spoke with Washington County Sheriff Dan Starry about crisis intervention training, opioid reentry programs, and support for mental health resources in our community.

Crisis Intervention Training for Cops

Afton Indivisible member Lucia Wroblewski served over 28 years as a St. Paul police officer and has a passion for good policing. She presented an inside look at coaching St. Paul police to deescalating mental health crises.
